Hi, I am Arnaud Jiang.

I studied physics at Sun Yat-sen University. This blog is where I keep track of what I am learning, building, and trying to understand more deeply.

My path has moved across several domains. I have worked on NLP algorithms, mainly training BERT-based models and delivering information extraction systems. I have also worked as a general-subject teacher. I am now an AI application engineer, focused on connecting model capabilities, software engineering, and real-world use cases into systems that are useful, reliable, and iterative.

The areas I keep coming back to include:

- AI and large language model applications
- Software development and engineering practice
- Computing architecture
- Theoretical physics
- Physics simulation
- Personal knowledge systems

At the moment, I am especially interested in:

- Plasma physics and nuclear fusion
- 3D Gaussian Splatting representations and interaction layers
- Multi-agent frameworks
- Reinforcement learning
- Investing in the U.S. technology sector

This site is not meant to be a polished portfolio of final answers. It is closer to a public notebook that keeps growing over time. I use it to organize reading notes, experiments, project reflections, and intermediate understandings, including questions that are still unfinished.

For me, writing is a way to turn scattered learning into something durable.
